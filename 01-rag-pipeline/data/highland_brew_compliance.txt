HIGHLAND BREW CO. - PRODUCT COMPLIANCE AND QUALITY STANDARDS DOCUMENT
Version 3.2 | January 2026

1. COMPANY OVERVIEW
Highland Brew Co. is a Scottish craft brewery based in Inverness, producing a range of 
ales, lagers, and seasonal beers for distribution across Scotland and the UK. All products 
are manufactured in accordance with UK food safety legislation and Scottish Environment 
Protection Agency (SEPA) guidelines.

2. ALLERGEN POLICY
All Highland Brew Co. products are produced in a facility that handles gluten-containing 
grains including barley, wheat, and oats. Our core lager range contains barley malt and 
is not suitable for people with gluten intolerance or coeliac disease. Our seasonal cider 
range is gluten-free and suitable for coeliacs. All allergens are declared on product 
labels in accordance with UK allergen labelling regulations.

4. QUALITY CONTROL PROCEDURES
All batches undergo microbiological testing prior to release. Testing is carried out by 
an accredited third party laboratory every two weeks. Acceptable limits are as follows:
- Total Viable Count: less than 100 cfu/ml
- Yeast and Mould: less than 10 cfu/ml
- Coliforms: not detected
Any batch failing these criteria is quarantined and destroyed. Results are logged in 
the Quality Management System and retained for 5 years.

5. PRODUCTION HYGIENE STANDARDS
The production facility is cleaned and sanitised daily using food-grade cleaning agents 
approved by the British Beer and Pub Association. Staff handling open products must wear 
PPE including hairnets, gloves, and protective clothing. The facility undergoes a full 
third party hygiene audit every six months. The most recent audit was completed in 
November 2025 and received a score of 97 out of 100.

6. TRACEABILITY AND RECALL PROCEDURES
Each batch is assigned a unique batch code printed on all packaging. In the event of a 
product recall, Highland Brew Co. can trace and withdraw all affected stock within 24 
hours. The recall procedure is tested annually. Key contacts for recall events are the 
Quality Manager and the Head of Operations. All recall events must be reported to Food 
Standards Scotland within 24 hours of identification.

7. ENVIRONMENTAL AND SUSTAINABILITY POLICY
Highland Brew Co. is committed to reducing its environmental impact. The brewery 
operates a water recycling system that reduces water consumption by 40% compared to 
industry average. Spent grain from the brewing process is donated to local farms as 
animal feed. The facility runs on 100% renewable energy sourced from Scottish wind farms.
All packaging is made from recycled materials and is fully recyclable.
