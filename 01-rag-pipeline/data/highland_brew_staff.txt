HIGHLAND BREW CO. - HEALTH, SAFETY AND STAFF POLICY
Version 2.1 | June 2025

1. HEALTH AND SAFETY RESPONSIBILITIES
The Health and Safety Officer at Highland Brew Co. is Angus Morrison.
All staff are responsible for maintaining a safe working environment.
Any accidents or near misses must be reported to the Health and Safety Officer
within 4 hours of the incident occurring.

2. TRAINING REQUIREMENTS
All new staff must complete the following training before working unsupervised:
- Food hygiene certificate Level 2: must be completed within first 2 weeks
- Manual handling training: must be completed on first day
- Fire safety training: must be completed within first week
- COSHH awareness: must be completed within first 2 weeks
Training records are held by HR and reviewed annually.

3. PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT
PPE is provided free of charge to all staff. Required PPE by area:
- Production floor: steel toe cap boots, hi-vis vest, hairnet, gloves
- Laboratory: lab coat, gloves, safety glasses
- Warehouse: steel toe cap boots, hi-vis vest
PPE must be worn at all times in designated areas. Failure to wear PPE is a 
disciplinary matter.

4. ANNUAL LEAVE POLICY
All full time staff are entitled to 28 days annual leave per year including bank holidays.
Part time staff receive annual leave on a pro rata basis.
Annual leave requests must be submitted at least 2 weeks in advance.
No more than 3 members of the production team may take leave simultaneously.
Unused annual leave may not be carried over beyond 31st March each year.

5. SICKNESS ABSENCE POLICY
Staff must notify their line manager by phone before 8am on the first day of absence.
Self certification is acceptable for absences up to 7 days.
A fit note from a GP is required for absences of 8 days or more.
Absences of more than 4 weeks trigger a formal return to work assessment.

6. DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURE
Stage 1: Verbal warning - remains on record for 6 months
Stage 2: Written warning - remains on record for 12 months
Stage 3: Final written warning - remains on record for 18 months
Stage 4: Dismissal
Gross misconduct may result in immediate dismissal without prior warnings.
